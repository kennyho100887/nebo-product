Outpatient Clinical Record  |  MRN AGL841399564  |  Milton, Jenny  |  DOB 02/06/1971
Chief complaint

71-year-old female presenting with bilateral lower extremity cramping and pain, sciatica, and constant numbness over the right lateral thigh with progression to the left lower extremity.

History of present illness

Patient reports 2-3 years of BLE cramping/pain that has gradually worsened. Describes constant numbness over R lateral thigh; Sx have slowly been progressing to LLE. Pain is worse with standing and ambulation, partially relieved by sitting and forward flexion.

Conservative management to date: has tried Advil, Tylenol, Diclofenac gel, and CBD oil w/o relief of SX. Reports no bowel or bladder dysfunction. No recent trauma.

- Page 1 of 2 -
Physical examination

Gait antalgic. Positive straight leg raise on the right at approximately 45 degrees. Decreased sensation L4-L5 dermatome on the right. Motor strength 4/5 in right extensor hallucis longus. Deep tendon reflexes symmetric.

Assessment & plan

Clinical picture consistent with neurogenic claudication and lumbar radiculopathy. Given progressive neurologic symptoms and failure of conservative management exceeding 6 weeks, requesting lumbar MRI without contrast for further evaluation.

Will consider referral for interventional pain management pending imaging results.
